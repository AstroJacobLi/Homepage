# Schechter Mass Function
The Pressâ€“Schechter formalism predicts that the number of objects with mass between <script type="math/tex">M</script> and <script type="math/tex">M+dM</script> is:

<script type="math/tex; mode=display"> N(M)dM=\frac{1}{\sqrt{\pi}}\left(1+{\frac{n}{3}}\right)\frac{\bar \rho}{M^2}\left(\frac{M}{M^\*}\right)^{{\left(3+n\right)/6}}\exp\left(-\left({\frac{M}{M^{*}}}\right)^{{\left(3+n\right)/3}}\right)dM</script>

where <script type="math/tex">{\bar {\rho }}</script> is the mean (baryonic and dark) matter density of the universe, $ n $ is the index of the power spectrum of the fluctuations in the early universe <script type="math/tex">P(k)\propto k^{n}</script>, and<script type="math/tex">M^*</script>is a critical mass above which structures will form.

# <script type="math/tex">D_{4000}</script> break

(http://adsabs.harvard.edu/full/1997A\%26A...325.1025P) and [http://www.galaxyzooforum.org/index.php?topic=1923.0] talked about <script type="math/tex">D_{4000} </script> break. Briefly speaking, <script type="math/tex">D_{4000}</script> break is the ratio between the average flux density in <script type="math/tex">[4050\AA,4250\AA]</script> and in <script type="math/tex">[3750\angstrom,3950\angstrom]</script>. We can see from Fig.1, the ratio of average flux density in this two area can be calculated easily.
